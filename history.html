From its humble beginnings as a small settlement in the Middle Ages, Valladolid has witnessed and played a leading role in key moments of Spanish's history. Capital of the Empire and Golden Age

Valladolid reached its peak during the Golden Age. In 1469, the Catholic Monarchs, Isabel and Ferdinand, were married at the Vivero Palace. Later, Philip II was born in the city and established it as the capital. During this period, Miguel de Cervantes lived in Valladolid and published the first edition of Don Quixote here in 1604. Tragically, Christopher Columbus died in Valladolid in 1506.

Modern Era

After the permanent transfer of the court to Madrid, the city went through a period of decline. It was not until the 19th century, with the arrival of the railway, that Valladolid began its industrial transformation, becoming an important manufacturing and communications center.
